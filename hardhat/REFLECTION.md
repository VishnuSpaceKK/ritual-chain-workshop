## Reflection: Public, Hidden, AI vs Human

What should be public: the bounty's existence, its reward, its rubric, the deadlines,
and who participated (addresses) — none of that creates an unfair advantage if known
upfront. What should stay hidden until judging is complete is the actual content of
each submission, since visibility before the window closes is exactly what enables
copying. AI should decide objective, rubric-based comparisons — consistency at
ranking many submissions against stated criteria, at a scale and speed no human judge
could match fairly. A human (the bounty owner) should retain the final call on
finalizing the winner, because rubrics can't anticipate every edge case, and an LLM
judging an AI-related bounty about its own judging mechanism is a case where human
oversight matters most. The ideal split, in short: humans set the rules and have final
veto, the protocol enforces secrecy mechanically rather than by trust, and AI handles
the repetitive comparative judgment in between.
